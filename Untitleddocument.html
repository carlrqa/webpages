                   <h2 class="text-3xl font-bold text-[#333333] mb-4">Theme: The Evolving Role of QA</h2>

                   <p class="text-lg text-gray-700">A major theme was the function of QA—moving from a "fixer" to a "partner" and focusing on systemic improvements over individual errors. This requires a shift in mindset for both QA and Operations.</p>

               <div class="grid grid-cols-1 lg:grid-cols-2 gap-6">

                       <h3 class="text-2xl font-bold text-[#333333] mb-4">How do we maintain QA independence and avoid being "The Fixer"?</h3>

                       <p class="text-gray-600 mb-6">How can QA maintain independence when they want to "jump in and fix"? The consensus focused on a consultative approach that builds capability in other teams.</p>

                       <div class="space-y-4">

                           <div class="w-full p-4 border-2 border-[#D9A404] rounded-lg shadow text-center bg-white">

                               <h4 class="font-bold text-lg text-[#333333]">1. Advise & Partner</h4>

                               <p class="text-gray-600">Facilitate process mapping, provide options, and explain regulatory requirements. Ghost-write first drafts, but don't own the final doc.</p>

                           </div>

                           <div class="text-3xl font-bold text-[#D9A404] text-center">↓</div>

                           <div class="w-full p-4 border-2 border-[#BFBDB6] rounded-lg shadow text-center bg-white">

                               <h4 class="font-bold text-lg text-[#333333]">2. Let Operations Decide</h4>

                               <p class="text-gray-600">Maintain independence by letting the operational team choose the path forward and own the fix.</p>

                           </div>

                           <div class="text-3xl font-bold text-[#BFBDB6] text-center">↓</div>

                           <div class="w-full p-4 border-2 border-[#8C7A6B] rounded-lg shadow text-center bg-white">

                               <h4 class="font-bold text-lg text-[#333333]">3. Enable Learning</h4>

                               <p class="text-gray-600">As the host noted, "if you fix it for them, they won't learn and it will be repeated." This approach prevents repeated errors.</p>

                           </div>

                       </div>

                   </div>

                 

                   <div class="bg-white rounded-lg shadow-lg p-6">

                       <h3 class="text-2xl font-bold text-[#333333] mb-4">How can QA engage people to reduce human errors?</h3>

                       <p class="text-gray-600 mb-6">The community strongly agreed that the focus should be on the system, not the person, to prevent recurrence.</p>

                       <ul class="list-disc pl-6 space-y-3 text-gray-700">

                           <li><strong class="text-[#333333]">Understand the True Root Cause:</strong> Avoid labelling "human error." Investigate if the process is unclear, the person is overworked, or the system is confusing.</li>

                           <li><strong class="text-[#333333]">Involve Users:</strong> Use workshops and involve users in process mapping and SOP writing to create easy-to-read, followable procedures.</li>

                           <li><strong class="text-[#333333]">Foster Autonomy:</strong> Give people input into how things are done and what processes will work. This reduces resistance and improves buy-in.</li>

                       </ul>

                       <h3 class="text-2xl font-bold text-[#333333] mb-4">How do we upskill QA for AI?</h3>

                       <p class="text-gray-600 mb-6">How do we prepare QA for AI beyond just a checklist? The key is treating AI like a new, powerful tool that still requires human oversight.</p>

                   <p class="text-lg text-gray-700">Specific regulatory questions highlighted the industry's focus on adapting to new guidelines like ICH E6 (R3) and navigating complex, nuanced compliance scenarios on a case-by-case basis.                <div class="grid grid-cols-1 lg:grid-cols-2 gap-6">

                       <h3 class="text-2xl font-bold text-[#333333] mb-4">How do you manage documentation reduction vs. compliance (GCP)?</h3>

                       <p class="text-gray-600 mb-6">When Operations wants to reduce documentation, QA's role is to ensure compliance. The consensus was not to just say "no," but to guide the process.</p>

                       <ul class="list-disc pl-6 space-y-3 text-gray-700">

                           <li><strong class="text-[#333333]">Critically Appraise:</strong> Don't just say no. Appraise their proposal against the regulations, not just your internal SOPs.</li>

                           <li><strong class="text-[#333333]">Reference ICH E6 (R3):</strong> The updated essential records section may well support their proposal for simplification.</li>

                           <li><strong class="text-[#333333]">Identify What Matters:</strong> Focus on minimum compliance and the source records needed to reconstruct the trial.</li>

                           <li><strong class="text-[#333333]">Explore Alternatives:</strong> Ask Operations to demonstrate how the task is done and suggest capturing info in a system (e.g., eQMS) or as metadata.</li>

                       </ul>

                   </div>

                   <div class="bg-white rounded-lg shadow-lg p-6 flex flex-col">

                       <h3 class="text-2xl font-bold text-[#333333] mb-4 text-center">Should patient eligibility issues be reported as serious breaches?</h3>

                       <p class="text-gray-600 mb-6 text-center">On the question of whether patient eligibility issues are serious breaches, the answer was a resounding...</p>

                       <div class="flex-grow flex items-center justify-center">

                           <p class="text-6xl md:text-8xl font-extrabold text-[#D9A404] text-center">"It Depends"</p>

                       </div>

                       <p class="text-gray-700 mt-6 text-center">The key is to assess each case individually against the definition, considering its impact on participant safety and data reliability, and whether it's a systemic issue. If detected *prior* to administering IMP, it may be a major PD, not a breach.</p>

                   </div>

                   <div class="bg-white rounded-lg shadow-lg p-6">

                       <h3 class="text-2xl font-bold text-[#333333] mb-4">Are Quality events and PD listings filed in the TMF (ICH E6 R3)?</h3>

                       <p class="text-gray-600 mb-6">Are Quality Event reports & PD listings to be filed in the TMF as per the new line item in ICH E6 R3?</p>

                       <ul class="list-disc pl-6 space-y-3 text-gray-700">

                           <li><strong class="text-[#333333]">Consensus: Yes.</strong> Study-related QE and PD listings do need to form part of the TMF.</li>

                           <li><strong class="text-[#333333]">Method: Signpost.</strong> The location (e.g., in the QMS) can be defined on the TMF Index. You don't necessarily have to file the report *directly* in the eTMF, but its location must be specified.</li>

                       </ul>

                   </div>

                   <div class="bg-white rounded-lg shadow-lg p-6">

                       <h3 class="text-2xl font-bold text-[#333333] mb-4">Do we check audit trails for non-compliant GLP method validations?</h3>

                       <p class="text-gray-600 mb-6">For GLP, if method validations are not required to be compliant, would you still check the audit trail as a de-risked approach?</p>

                       <ul class="list-disc pl-6 space-y-3 text-gray-700">

                           <li><strong class="text-[#333333]">If not GLP compliant, QA does not check.</strong></li>

                           <li>If method validation for dose formulation *is* GLP, then yes, audit trails are checked.</li>

                           <li>Some would only look at the audit trail if a major compliance issue was spotted, to deep-dive the root cause.</li>

                       </ul>

           for (let i = 0; i < maxRetries; i++) {

               try {

                   const response = await fetch(url, options);

                   if (response.status !== 429) {

                       return response;

                   }

               } catch (error) {

                 

               }

               const delay = Math.pow(2, i) * 1000 + Math.random() * 1000;

               await new Promise(resolve => setTimeout(resolve, delay));
